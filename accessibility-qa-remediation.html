Accessibility QA & Remediation: Bringing 9 Courses to Full Compliance

Role: Accessibility Consultant | Client: A Public University (Confidential)

Client Challenge

The university needed a batch of online courses brought into compliance with accessibility standards ahead of institutional review. Course content had accumulated over multiple semesters without a consistent accessibility standard, resulting in a wide range of starting points — some courses were close to compliant, others had significant structural and content issues. Each course contained between 40 and 100 files requiring remediation, with issues ranging from minor to severe.

My Process

I ran a two-layer QA process: automated scoring via Blackboard Ally combined with manual review against the Quality Matters (QM) rubric and custom internal criteria, to catch issues automated tools miss, like logical reading order, meaningful link text, and appropriate use of ARIA.

Issues were triaged by severity, from severe to major, and by type, so remediation effort could be prioritized where it would have the greatest impact on usability for students using assistive technology. After remediation, I re-scored each course to confirm measurable improvement with no regressions.

Common Issue Categories Addressed

  • Heading structure & navigation (improper heading hierarchy, non-semantic markup)
  • Document accessibility (PDFs and Word docs lacking tags, reading order, or proper structure)
  • Color contrast failures
  • Images and graphics missing meaningful alt text

Why It Matters

Ally scores are a proxy for real barriers: a low score means students using screen readers, keyboard navigation, or other assistive technology are likely hitting broken or confusing experiences. Bringing every course to 99+ meant closing that gap consistently across the board, not just fixing the easiest wins.

This work also positioned the university ahead of ADA Title II compliance deadlines for public entities, which require web and course content to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA.
